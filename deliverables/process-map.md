# Henderson & Associates — Client Intake Process Map

| # | Step | Automate / Keep Human / Hybrid | Justification |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Incoming call received by Tamika (receptionist answers) | Keep Human | First voice contact sets tone for a frightened or injured caller; a human answer reduces abandonment and starts the relationship on a personal note. |
| 2 | Tamika prints intake form (Word doc, done each morning) | Automate | Generating a fresh form daily is pure clerical overhead; a digital intake form or CRM screen eliminates this entirely within the $2k budget. |
| 3 | Tamika collects basics: name, phone, accident type, date of incident | Hybrid | Tamika can continue collecting info verbally, but the data should flow directly into a digital form (not a paper printout) so it reaches Clio without re-entry later. |
| 4 | Tamika checks statute of limitations against wall chart | Automate | SOL rules are deterministic and jurisdiction-specific; a simple lookup table or intake script can flag eligibility instantly and eliminate the risk of Tamika misreading the chart. |
| 5 | Tamika routes call to Maria or takes a message if Maria is unavailable | Keep Human | Routing is a simple human judgment call Tamika can make; the fix here is not automation but a reliable callback queue (see step 6). |
| 6 | Maria (or firm) calls prospect back — target 24 hours, actual 2–3 days | Hybrid | Maria must make the call herself (Maria's explicit position, Laura's constraint), but an automated reminder or ticket alert triggered at the 2-hour mark would prevent the multi-day slip that costs the firm clients. |
| 7 | Maria conducts screening call (8–10 structured questions: accident location, fault, witnesses, injuries, treatment status) | Hybrid | The questions are structured and repeatable — an AI-assisted intake form sent to the prospect before the call could pre-populate answers, letting Maria focus on judgment rather than data collection. |
| 8 | Maria assesses case viability ("knows within two minutes") | Keep Human | Legal merit assessment requires attorney-adjacent judgment and 15 years of pattern recognition; this is exactly what Maria is paid for and where she adds irreplaceable value. |
| 9 | Maria provides extended emotional support to non-viable callers | Keep Human | These are injured and distressed people — Laura explicitly said any client-facing AI must have Maria's buy-in, and Maria's position is clear; however, a post-call flag could prompt Maria to set a time limit before it escalates. |
| 10 | Maria manually searches 15-year conflicts-of-interest spreadsheet (~10 min per prospect) | Automate | This is a high-stakes, rules-based lookup with real disbarment risk if missed; automating it against a structured database (integrated with Clio) reduces both time and malpractice exposure — this is the strongest automation case in the intake. |
| 11 | Maria enters intake data into Clio — contact info, accident details, injuries (~15–20 min) | Automate | Pure data re-entry from information already collected in steps 3 and 7; if intake is captured digitally upstream, it can flow directly into Clio via its API, saving Maria roughly 15–20 min per prospect. |
| 12 | Maria sends retainer agreement via DocuSign | Keep Human | Laura described this as "our one technology win" — the step is already working well and changing it creates risk with no upside. |
| 13 | Laura reviews and formally accepts the case | Keep Human | Bar rules and the constraints doc explicitly note that the client relationship is not established until Laura accepts; this is a legal decision that must stay with the attorney. |
| 14 | Maria makes 7-day post-retainer check-in call | Hybrid | The call itself stays human (Maria's explicit requirement), but an automated calendar trigger and reminder ensures the call actually happens; currently the 7-day call is the only one that's reliably made. |
| 15 | Maria makes 14-day post-retainer check-in call | Hybrid | Same rationale as step 14 — the 14-day call is "fifty-fifty" by Laura's account; an automated prompt to Maria's task queue would bring it to near-100% with no client-facing AI involved. |
| 16 | Maria makes 30-day post-retainer check-in call | Hybrid | The 30-day call "falls off" entirely by Laura's account; automated scheduling inside Clio (which the firm already uses) would surface this as a task and prevent client dissatisfaction and potential lost referrals. |
